[Initial Impressions] The Shoe Mart: Alden Ravello Indys

December 30, 2020

Background

As many know, getting on a list for rare colors of Alden shell can be difficult and once you’re on a list there are tales of people waiting for upwards of 3-5 years for a chance to buy the shoes they want. I seem to have lucked out a bit. I got on The Shoe Mart’s (TSM) list back in December of 2018 and got the email that I was at the front of the line for a model I was interested in in early June of this hellish year.

I emailed back and forth a bit with Dylan-rose from TSM about sizing, lead-time, and confirming the specs of the boot. Overall it was very straight forward. The only hiccup was TSM takes checks for these orders, mailed to their warehouse in Connecticut. 100% of the cost, up-front, by check, via snail-mail. I confirmed with a couple of folks that yes indeed this is how it works and it totally legit before mailing off a nearly $1k paper check.

Fast forward 6 months and I get an email that my order status has been updated with tracking info and the game is on.

Specs

These are identical to the standard TSM Color #8 Indy boot (which seem to be gone from their site at the time of writing) except the color is Ravello.

  • Uppers: Ravello Shell Cordovan
  • Last: Trubalance
  • Eyelets: 5 eyelets, 4 speedhooks brown
  • Welt: 360 pre-stitched split welt
  • Edging: Antique
  • Sole: Commando half sole and heel

The main difference from other Indy boots is the 360 degree pre-stitched welt and commando sole. The classic models tend to have a 270 storm welt and the Neoprene cork sole with the Thomas heel. All shell models have a handstiched moc-toe instead of the standard machine stitch on other leathers.

I’m a huge fan of the roomy Trubalance last already and the commando soles are a dream. I think I prefer the 270 welt look to 360 but it’s far from a deal-breaker for me. I just really like the butt on the 270 welts.

Sizing

Nothing wild here, I went 1/2 size down from my brannock: 8.5D US to 8D US. The Trubalance last is quite roomy still so I’ll have to wear thick socks but I do that with all of my boots anyway. If you are looking for a comfortable boot I really think the Trubalance last is the way to go. Classic 405 or 403 Indys are some of the best boots out there and so many people confirm that they’re one of the most comfortable pairs of shoes they own.

Wrap-up

My main concern with these is that I find shell to be cold. It’s a very chilly leather for whatever reason and an issue that I already have with my stitched boots is that they aren’t very warm in the winter. Hopefully the roomier Trubalance last and my thick wool socks will help. If not, I have my eye on a pair of Edward Green Galways with shearling lining.

Finally, Indys don’t have a gusseted tongue and a common issue is that the tongue slides off to the side as a result. At some point I will ship these off to a cobbler to stitch the tongue in place. But for now, I can’t wait to wear these out and get the patina going. I don’t wear boots all that often since I don’t need to and shoes are more comfortable but these will definitely be getting the call.
