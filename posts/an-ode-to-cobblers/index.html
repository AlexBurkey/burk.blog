An Ode to Cobblers

August 11, 2020

Cobblers are what make the world go ‘round in the world of stitched footwear. They can skillfully install after-market features like toe-taps or rubber sole protectors. Cobblers are the ones that replace the shitty stock leather soles with glorious JR soles or put real Dainite on your boots.

They’re the ones that give a beloved pair a new lease on life long after the production run has ended.

Plus, there’s so much more to cobbling than new heels and soles. A good cobbler knows shoes inside and out and can do things like replace a broken heel counter or hide awful gashes in the uppers.

Removing water stains and amazing polishing jobs are also game.

You can spend your whole life caring for your shoes at home with more conditioners and polishes than your bank account should support, and you might never be as good as someone who does it for a living. Make use of the specialization and the knowledge someone gets from cleaning, polishing, and fixing hundreds if not thousands of shoes. They know more than the internet and they know more than you.

While it may not be financially viable to spend $150 for a new sole on a pair of $225 shoes, I think the romance of high quality footwear is with cobblers. The magic is in the repairs done to keep your long time favorites in tip-top shape, not the unboxing of a new pair. It may be hard for us who love this hobby and shoes so much, but I think our money is much better spent on repairs instead of more pairs.
