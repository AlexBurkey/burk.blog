Collection 2022

January 16, 2022

I haven’t taken stock of my collection in a little while so since I’ve been stuck inside with COVID I took some pictures and decided to write this up about my main rotation.

The grails of my collection. Alden Indy boots in Ravello shell cordovan from The Shoe Mart. These frankly have the most aggressive sole aside from my hiking boots, so I wear them when it snows. Since I’m work from home they don’t get much wear but the Trubalance last is fantastic and I like the commando sole. The only thing I would change is I’d rather a 270 welt rather than 360.

The workhorse. Rider Boot Co. Fritz Chelsea boots in 750 Reverso leather. Probably my favorite boots. I don’t like futzing with laces and with a shoe horn these go on super quickly and easily. If I have to pick one pair of boots these are the ones I pick.

The veg-tan leather is showing nice patina, compare them to when they were new (below). I don’t baby these at all, the only draw back is the soles don’t do well in snow so if that’s a factor I opt for my Indy boots instead.

A pair I haven’t had much of a chance to wear recently. Color #8 Alden Leisure Handsewn Loafers. I tend to break these out for date nights and such for 3 seasons out of the year. The shell is still fairly stiff and again with WFH I don’t get many chances to wear shoes unless they’re okay in the house. This pair is not since they have metal toe taps.

Another one of my favorites. Alden snuff suede LHS loafer. Obviously best for spring and summer seasons so they haven’t been worn much in the last 6 months. But these are ol’ reliable for the warmer days. Snuff suede is really nice and these are my fiance’s favorites so they also get the call for date nights.

Rancourt Natural CXL loafers. Grabbed these during the crazy sales that Rancourt was running. They aren’t my ideal make-up, I’d prefer the LH sole which is softer but still heeled which I like. Earlier this year I was trying to wear them a bunch to wear out the sole, but again, haven’t been super successful.

Dayton Service Boots in charcoal nubuck. I haven’t worn these in quite a while, but I still love them. The v-tread is fairly worn down and I tend to pick my chelsea boots instead if the weather is nice. The leather is fantastic and I really hope that Dayton’s quality gets back to where it was when I purchased these.

My oldest pair in rotation: Oak Street Bootmakers blucher mocs in peanut suede. Going big on brown suede train but I love these shoes. The laces make them easier to wear with different socks and such and the camp soles mean they can deal with just about anything. I’ve barely ever cared for them other than brushing and at over 8 years of ownership they still look fantastic. This is the pair I show people when they’re worried about suede being delicate or getting ruined from water.

I have some other pairs hanging around but they don’t really get worn at all, or are still hiding in boxes after the move. That’s the main set I have right now though!
