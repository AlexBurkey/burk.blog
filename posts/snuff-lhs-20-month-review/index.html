20 Month Review: Alden Unlined Snuff Suede LHS

May 23, 2021

Background

These bad bois are probably my favorite shoes. I was no stranger to snuff suede before this pair (see below), but I always admired this model from afar. In late 2019 I decided to bite the bullet and headed into Alden of DC to see what they had. Helpful as ever I was able to determine my size, but the shade of snuff on the pair they had in-store was a bit too brown for me. They happily ordered another pair for me from the factory and when they arrived I made the trek back in to try them on, check out the color, and ultimately, bring my new babies home!

Specs

  • Uppers: CF Stead Snuff Janus Calf
  • Last: Van
  • Welt: 360 flat welt
  • Edging: Antique
  • Sole: waterlock
  • Eyelets: lol
  • Lining: lol

Just the classic unlined LHS pattern using CF Stead’s full-grain Janus Calf leather. The uppers and wole were supple and soft directly out of the box, they are more like slippers than GYW shoes. The waterlock soles are my favorite of any shoes: they were quick to break in, are fairly durable, and the texture means they’re slightly tacky out of the box so I never dealt with the dreaded new leather sole slickness.

Initially I wasn’t fully sold on the 360 welt. I just think 270 welts look fantastic in general and Alden has some loafer models with 270 welts that are awesome. Now I think the 360 welt is perfect as it leans into the casual vibe these shoes have.

Sizing

I measure 8.5D US on Brannock and these are 7.5D, as are my other pair of LHS loafers. The main thing to look out for with sizing is the strap. If your instep is high the strap isn’t going to loosen up too much as it’s stitched all the way across. Other than the strap sizing is quite forgiving but, these are loafers so I recommend being able to try on multiple sizes before settling

Care

I’ve done nothing besides brush and use shoe trees. As I’ve mentioned in my care guide, suede is incredibly resilient. If you’re okay with how it ages, suede is a fantastic leather and CF Stead’s iterations of it are exactly what you want. The tannage is widely available and a relatively cheaper leather but I’ve not had any quality issues to speak of. Below you can see a picture of them on their maiden voyage and a detail shot of how the toes look now.

I’ve worn these at the beach and at bars as well as my cushy office job, so they’ve picked up sand, salt, alcohol, and office chair bumps. Suede will burnish a little and can pick up some minor stains but I think the shoes look better for it and I’m too lazy to keep my shoes looking pristine. No protector sprays. No soaps, shampoos, or cleaners. No conditioners.

Just shoe trees and a horsehair brush.

Wrap Up

Alden is well known for a few unique patterns: the Indy boot, tassel loafers, and the LHS loafer. The LHS pattern might be the greatest of these, I think it’s the most under-rated.

They are my most-worn shoe since I’ve bought them and the wear shows it for my lifestyle. I don’t put tons of miles on them working in an office most of the time (and now from home) but the rubber heel lifts show wear and the bottom layer of leather in the heel stack shows minor water damage. The toes have been burnished a little and show wear. The heel stamps are surprisingly intact.

They hold one of the special locations not on my shoe rack for storage. They sit on a clothing rack that my grandfather owned that holds a seasonal jacket and whatever other pair of shoes I’m into at the moment. Right now I really like having both pairs of LHS loafers there.

Thanks for reading!
