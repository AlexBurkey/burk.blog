Suede is NOT Delicate

June 16, 2022

Myth: suede is delicate.

I don’t know how the myth got started, it’s entirely false. Real suede is an incredibly durable leather. It doesn’t show surface scratches or stains very much. It can get literally soaked and bounce right back. It’s incredibly easy to care for; it’s receptive to neglect and papering.

These blucher mocassins from Oak Street Bootmakers are over 7 years old and they’ve been worn quite hard. Even in snow and slush. I’ve never done more than brush them and use a suede eraser on occasion.

They are obviously not spotless, but I like them that way. They’re a casual shoe and having a worn-in look suits them. And again, this is over 7 years of neglect.

No conditioner.
No water-proof spray.
Just brushing and a suede eraser. Not even a suede brush!

I was recently on a trip to DC and we got caught in an absolute downpour. I was wearing my trusty Alden LHS loafers in snuff suede.

Alden’s snuff suede is Janus calf suede from C.F.Stead. C.F. Stead also makes a Repello suede which is treated with scotchguard and is water-proof out of the box. Janus calf by contrast, is not. It is still a high quality suede though and it can take a beating.

The picture above looks quite dire. The shoes are entirely soaked through. The lighting isn’t great but, to give you a sense of the proper color, you can see a bit of the dry blucher mocs in the background on the right.

The reports of their death were greatly exaggerated.

After flying back home I let them sit in shoe trees for a few days. Normally I’d recommend letting them rest on their side with newspaper stuffed in (and regularly changed) to dry but by the time I had gotten back they were already dry.

Then I washed them in the sink with a literal dish brush and dawn dish soap.

Read that again.

In the sink.
With dish soap.

No fancy shampoos.
No fancy brushes.

This time I let them sit for a few days, on their side, with newspaper stuffed in them and changed a couple of times.

Once properly dried I swapped the paper for shoe trees, brushed them with a horsehair brush, and finally used some Saphir Renovateur spray on them and voila!

Suede isn’t delicate. That’s not even the first time I’ve soaked a pair of snuff suede in DC!

I hope this provides a bit of perspective for folks. You don’t need to worry about suede getting wet, it’s very hardy. And if you do want to really baby it, I recommend an actual suede brush, a spray conditioner, and a nanoprotector/waterproofing spray. That simple care routine will keep them looking pristine for a very long time.

Suede looks better with a bit of character anyway though.

Cheers!
